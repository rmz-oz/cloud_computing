Topic 1: Introduction to Cloud Computing

1 · Service Availability & The Nines

A key metric for cloud services is uptime — expressed as a percentage of the year the service is available. Higher availability means less downtime.

Goal: Internet services aim for 99.999% availability ("five nines") — only 5.26 minutes of downtime per year. Automating routine operations is key to achieving this.

3 · Traditional Hardware Purchase Model

In the traditional model, companies buy servers based on predicted future demand. This leads to two persistent problems:

Over-provisioning
  • Buy large hardware upfront
  • At normal times only 15-20% of capacity is used
  • Resources are wasted and expensive
  • "Buy a large size, then eat to fill it"
Under-provisioning
  • Demand spikes exceed capacity
  • Unhappy customers during peak traffic
  • Opportunity cost — lost sales
  • Hardware takes weeks to procure
The problem: Servers are bought based on future demand forecasts, not real-time demand. During peak periods (e.g., holiday season), existing hardware is insufficient. During off-peak, 85% sits idle.

4 · Operation Dynamics

Web traffic is highly variable and depends on:

  • Time of day (peak vs. off-peak hours)
  • Season (holiday shopping spikes)
  • Marketing & promotional campaigns
  • Viral popularity or news events
  • Instant customer demand surges

Because demand is unpredictable, over-provisioning is unavoidable in the traditional model. The ideal solution: provision infrastructure that matches real demand dynamically — scale up when needed, scale down when not.

5 · Cloud Computing Solution

Cloud Computing provides on-demand access to computing resources (servers, storage, networking) over the internet, billed only for what you use. This eliminates over-provisioning and enables instant scaling.

Service Models

IaaS

Infrastructure as a Service. Raw VMs, storage, networking. You manage OS and up. (AWS EC2, Azure VMs)

PaaS

Platform as a Service. Managed runtime + middleware. You deploy code only. (Heroku, AWS Elastic Beanstalk)

SaaS

Software as a Service. Fully managed apps. You just use them. (Gmail, Salesforce, Dropbox)

Key Cloud Benefits

  • Elasticity: Scale up or down automatically based on demand
  • Pay-as-you-go: Pay only for resources consumed
  • No upfront capital: No hardware purchase needed
  • High availability: Providers guarantee SLA uptime (e.g., 99.99%)
  • Global reach: Deploy in data centers worldwide instantly

Operations Unit Responsibilities

  • Keep Dev, Test, and Production environments technically adequate
  • Share software and hardware resources across multiple projects efficiently
  • Maintain architectural standards compliance
  • Coordinate across technical operation units
  • Facilitate smooth transitions to new technologies
  • Meet end-user expectations through technical support
